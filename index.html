Marc Márquez

"You never know what will happen in the future so the most important thing is to enjoy the moment."

Marc Márquez Alentà is a Spanish Grand Prix motorcycle road racer and one of the most successful motorcycle racers of all time. Born in Cervera, Catalonia, Spain, on 17th February 1993, Marc already has eight Grand Prix world championships to his name – six of which are in the premier MotoGP class. Márquez races for Honda's factory team since his MotoGP debut in 2013.

On 10th November 2013, Marc Márquez became the youngest MotoGP World Champion in history, at just 20 years of age. Marc's 83 Grand Prix victories are the fourth highest of all time. His most successful circuit is Sachsenring, Germany, where he has won eleven times.

Marc was consistently breaking record after record, and his list of increadible acheivements appeared to be steadily increasing, up until the first race of 2020. In Jerez, he fell and broke his arm; a serious injury requiring surgery and a long recovery.

A list of records that Marc currently holds can be found on Wikipedia.

Marc perservered in his recovery journey for a long year of physical rehabilitation, and overcame every obstacle to work his way back to competition strength. After 9 months out of action he stepped back into the spotlight in the 3rd round of 2021. He quickly built back his confidence and came to win the 8th round of 2021 at his favourite Sachsenring circuit.

Marc was already inspirational in his world-class talent and determination, but this incredible comeback puts him amongst some of the world's greatest athletes; sporting heroes who make their return after battling serious, potentially career-ending injuries to continue persuing their passion.

The incredible strength of character to persevere through the devastation of injury, and come back on top is nothing short of awe-inspiring, and goes to show the power of belief and commitment.
